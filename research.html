Research areas

Work is organized around four intersecting themes — from causal methods and mixture modelling to environmental global health and policy-relevant evaluation in low-resource settings.

Causal Inference

G-computation, inverse probability weighting, and mediation analysis for policy-relevant causal questions in epidemiology.

Environmental and Social Exposome

BKMR, WQS, and novel methods for multi-exposure chemical mixture effects on health outcomes.

Environmental Global Health

Air pollution, chemical stressors, and their impacts on maternal–child health and non-communicable diseases.

Policy Evaluation & Environmental Justice

Evaluating policies and programs with an environmental justice lens — who bears environmental risks and who benefits from interventions — alongside applied work on childhood development, birth outcomes, developmental delays, and child mortality in low-resource South Asian settings.
